The History of the Myth

Ngatoroirangi is a legendary tohunga (priest/navigator) of the Te Arawa waka who traveled from Hawaiki to Aotearoa (New Zealand). His myth beautifully explains the origins of New Zealand's volcanic and geothermal activity, focusing on a perilous journey where he and his sisters summoned the heat of the earth to save his life.

The Myth

The powerful priest Ngatoroirangi froze in a blizzard atop Mount Tongariro. He screamed across the Pacific Ocean to his sisters, Kuiwai and Haungaroa, for help. They sent underground fire deities to save him. As these deities tunneled toward the mountain, they burst through the earth, permanently creating New Zealand's volcanoes and hot springs.
